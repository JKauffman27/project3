My Top 5 Games

1.) Hotline Miami 1 & 2

Genre: Top-Down Shooter

Personal Score: 9.5/10

Favorite Part:

Summary:I could unironically ramble for hours on hours about how much I love this game. The unique aesthetic, the heart-pumping gameplay, the club-esque music, the storytelling. This whole game is truly a one-of-a-kind experience. The best description I can give is that it goes from being an adrenaline rush to being a quiet graveyard in the matter of seconds.

2.) Bioshock

Genre: FPS, Horror

Personal Score: 9/10

Favorite Part:

Summary:To this day, Bioshock remains quite possibly the most interesting game I have ever played. The setting is both calming and peaceful, yet eerie and chilling. The incredible plot devices, character backgrounds, stories, world-building. Bioshock (at least the first and original) is something that I think most game developers of modern should look upon for inspiration on how to do both survival horror and storytelling.

3.) Left 4 Dead 2

Genre: FPS, Zombies

Personal Score: 10/10

Favorite Part:

Summary:This is the pinnacle of multiplayer experiences. Most modern games made today don't have nearly the level of complexity that this old game does. The levels are procedurally edited to fit the current situation of the players. Have a lot of med-kits and ammo? Here comes a big horde and a Tank. Have a whole lot of nothing, and someone's dead? Here's some supply. The game rapidly changes and becomes a new, fresh experience over hundreds of playthroughs. And of course, there are plenty of modded campaigns to play as well.

4.) Super Mario Galaxy

Genre: Platformer

Personal Score: 9/10

Favorite Part:

Summary:Yes, the introduction to the game is still my favorite part. As a whole, Super Mario Galaxy would just be another Mario game if not for certain things that differ slightly. Some of the music tracks are much slower than traditional Mario OST, the story is a lot more involved rather than just being "Save the Princess," and the level design is so unique and fun that the game can be played over and over again like it was the first time doing so.

5.) Outlast Series

Genre: Horror

Personal Score: 8.5/10

Favorite Part:

Summary:Not a game for the faint of heart, mind you. Outlast is a genre-defining horror experience, one that truly pushes the boundaries of games in its genre and allowed for innovation on the formula. Both games have very dark and extreme themes, mostly surrounding religion and human experimentation. The recently released Outlast Trials also help to push the series forward, completely innovating on the multiplayer survival-horror experience.
